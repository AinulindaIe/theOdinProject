Lasagna!

An overview of how to make an lasagna - Feeds 4 people (at least!)

  • 500 g minced meat (beef)
  • 4 cans tomatoes
  • 3 tsp tomato paste
  • 3 large onions
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 250 ml broth (any kind)
  • 250 ml mornay sauce
  • 1-2 packages of pasta plates
  • cheese for topping of the lasagna
  1. Finely dice the onions, and thinly slice garlic. Put aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pot. Fry the minced meat in smaller portions* till it is golden brown. Transfer browned meat to a bowl
  3. In the same pot add onions (and oil if need be). Stir them until they become slightly translucent. Add garlic and let them simmer for about a minute. Add tomato paste and stir. add canned tomatoes.
  4. Add broth. Let it simmer for half an hour - stiring occasionally.
  5. Add back the minced meat. Let it come back to temperature, while you season it to taste with pepper, salt, and italian herbs (e.g thyme).
  6. Preheat oven to 200 degrees celcius
  7. In a ovenproof dish alternate layers of beef-tomato sauce, pasta plates, mornay sauce. For the final layer top pastaplates with mornay sauce and cheese.
  8. Pop the dish in the oven and bake for 45 min, or until the cheese starts to bubble and brown.
  9. Take the dish out of the oven and let it cool for half and hour on the stovetop.

* - It is important not to crowd the bottom of the pot, as that tends to boil the meat instead of frying it. Depending on the potsize 125g is usually a good split to ensure it doesn't boil
